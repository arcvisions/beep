Na Bola Katha

This is a tale of a wife who blindly kept on idolizing her toxic husband until she realizes the true picture.The point is truth cannot be hidden for long, fabricated personalities can never thrive happily for long.

The entire story is a consequence of growing in a patriarchic family and a society, where males are forced to become inhumane and females are taught blindly to accept all their inhumane deeds with a chauvinistic justification. This bars female from their analyzing power. They are always asked to stay devoted to their male partner even if he is toxic, from a very tender age. The one who suddenly tries to break all these inappropriate setups is regarded as disgraceful and questioned.Similarly, the female protagonist here starts admiring her male chauvinistic husband for not having a proper education in gender equality.However, as time grows she started reading in between the lines and that's how the real picture of her husband came out. Dealing with an unfulfilled married life for years and a husband who disrespects her by cheating her repeatedly makes her dead from within. One fine day she gathers the courage to choose her heart's need and decides to remove all the disrespectful ties with her husband and leaves his place to live a life in a hope of learning to feel alive once again in her own ability. We have seen an imbalance in partnership in every next household is the reason behind many unsuccessful marriages.A healthy married life can only take place when both the partners happily learn to accept equality.

Beep & Colorbar is a studio which produces shorts films, feature films highlighting human emotion management, women empowerment, and related psychological issues that are often ignored in our society.
